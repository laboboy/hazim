MR297 Paddy Growth & Derivative Analysis

Applying calculus to agronomy: Use the derivative \( f'(x) \) to determine the optimal day for Nitrogen fertilizer application based on the Crop Growth Rate.

The Bioscience Problem

To maximize the yield of MARDI's MR297 paddy variety, farmers need to know when the crop is growing at its fastest rate. Total dry biomass accumulation follows an S-shaped Logistic Growth Curve.

While the original function \( f(x) \) tells us the total mass, calculating its derivative \( f'(x) \) gives us the Crop Growth Rate (CGR) in grams per square meter per day. Finding the peak CGR reveals the optimal window for applying top-dressing fertilizer (like Urea). Data profile simulated based on standard MARDI MR297 field trial patterns.

1. Total Biomass Function f(x)

f(x) = L / (1 + e-k(x - x₀))
  • x: Days After Transplanting (DAT)
  • L: Max Biomass Limit (1600 g/m²)
  • k: Growth Constant (0.12)
  • x₀: Day of maximum growth (Day 55)

2. Crop Growth Rate (Derivative) f'(x)

f'(x) = [L · k · e-k(x - x₀)] / [1 + e-k(x - x₀)]²

This derivative calculates the slope of the biomass curve at any given day. It tells us exactly how many grams of new mass the crop is adding per day.
